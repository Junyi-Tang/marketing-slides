Identification logic
Treatment is exogenous: firms did not choose to lose MA accountability. IFRS imposed the change.
Why the control group works
Never-reporters face the same IFRS transition but lose nothing specific. Any post-shock difference is attributable to MA accountability alone.

Matching design6 / 12
Propensity score matching
Match similar counterfactuals before estimating treatment effects.
Probit selection model
\[\text{MA reporting} = \beta_0 + \beta_1 X + \varepsilon \quad \text{(Eq. 2)}\]
MA reporting is the pre-IFRS choice to report MA, and \(X\) are firm characteristics. The estimated propensity score is \(\hat{p}(X_i) = \hat{\beta}_0 + \hat{\beta}_1 X_i\).
Tuning parameters
5 nearest neighbors with replacement — reduces influence of individual control observations
Caliper = 0.09 — strict distance cutoff to reject poor matches
Common support enforced — treated firms outside the control p-score range are dropped

DiD design7 / 12
Difference-in-differences
Compare pre/post changes across matched groups.
Formal specification
\[\text{Treatment effect} = \{E[Y_{\text{post}}|\text{Treat},p(X)] - E[Y_{\text{pre}}|\text{Treat},p(X)]\}\] \[- \{E[Y_{\text{post}}|\text{Control},p(X)] - E[Y_{\text{pre}}|\text{Control},p(X)]\} \quad \text{(Eq. 1)}\]
Inference
Standard errors clustered at industry level — accounts for heteroskedasticity and within-group correlation
DiD run conditionally on matched sample — treatment effects come from comparing similar counterfactuals only
Design sketch
Reverse interpretation note
Treatment is the loss of accountability. All effects must be read in reverse: a negative DiD \(\hat{\beta}\) means MA accountability had a positive effect.
Identifying assumptions8 / 12
Parallel trends
Test that treatment and control share the same pre-trend.
Result
TR × TI insignificant across all five outcomes (p > .13)
Parallel trends hold. No diverging pre-IFRS trajectories detected. Results replicated under full ITSA model.
Why it matters
If the two groups diverged before IFRS, post-shock differences cannot be attributed to the treatment.
How it is tested — ITSA
The DiD model is augmented with a continuous time trend TI and all interactions. The pre-trend test focuses on TR × TI — a diverging pre-IFRS trend between groups.
Credibility / robustness9 / 12
Alternative estimators and endogeneity
Three robustness strategies, one endogeneity control.
Entropy balancing
Reweights controls to match treated firms on distributional moments.
Applied on moments 1–2 (mean, variance) and 1–3 (+ skewness) with strict tolerance = 0.10. Results replicated in both cases.
Synthetic control
Optimal weighting of control firms to mimic each treated firm's pre-IFRS trajectory.
Weights determined by pre-intervention outcome values and covariates. Outperforms equal-weighted controls. Results replicated.
Strategic selection (endogeneity)
Firms knew IFRS was coming — could they have gamed their reporting?
Controlled via Inverse Mills Ratio. Exclusion restriction: industry peer-weighted MA reporting. IMR non-significant across all outcomes — no widespread strategic selection.
Verdict
All three alternative estimators replicate the main results. The core finding is not an artifact of the PSM+DiD choice.
