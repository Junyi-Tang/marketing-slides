Consequences of Marketing Asset Accountability — Mechanism

Why does reporting the financial value of brands and customer relationships change managerial focus, investor judgment, and stock price quality?

The central mechanism is straightforward: once marketing assets become visible in financial reporting, they become harder for managers and investors to ignore.

Mechanism
1 / 7
Mechanism overview

MA accountability operates through two linked channels

The paper defines marketing asset accountability as the continuous valuation and reporting of marketing assets to financial markets. That disclosure then works through an internal channel and an external channel.

Input

Financial reporting makes the value of brands, customer relationships, and other marketing assets explicit.

Internal channel

Measurement requirements shift managerial attention toward long-term value creation and away from purely current-period outcomes.

External channel

Investors receive a forward-looking benchmark for evaluating future cash flows, risk, and firm value.

Mechanism claim: disclosure does not only inform outsiders. It also changes what the firm itself measures, monitors, and manages.
2 / 7
Mechanism 1 · Internal managerial path

External accountability redirects managerial attention toward long-term marketing value

Step 1Firms must continuously value marketing assets rather than only record current marketing expense.
→
Step 2This creates more systematic measurement of lagged marketing benefits and asset-based value creation.
Step 3Managers gain clearer visibility into long-term cash flow drivers, not just short-term profitability.
→
OutcomeMarketing decisions become less myopic and long-term marketing efficiency improves.

Without MA accountability

  • marketing appears mainly as an expense,
  • current-period profitability dominates evaluation,
  • short-term focus becomes easier to justify.

With MA accountability

  • marketing assets enter the reporting system,
  • long-term value becomes measurable,
  • managers are pushed to think in asset terms.
The mechanism is an attention-and-measurement mechanism: what gets formally valued becomes more central to managerial decision making.
3 / 7
Mechanism 2 · Investor risk path

MA accountability lowers investor uncertainty about future returns from marketing

Core logic

Investors can already see marketing spending, but spending alone does not reveal the long-term value created by that spending. MA reporting narrows that information gap.

SignalReported MA values reveal management’s estimate of future marketing-generated value.
→
Risk effectLess uncertainty about asset quality and governance reduces perceived investment risk.
Financing effectLower uncertainty reduces the required return demanded by investors.
→
OutcomeCost of capital, especially cost of equity, falls.
4 / 7
Mechanism 3 · Stock price informativeness

Even imperfect valuations can improve market pricing by anchoring expectations

Tension

Marketing asset values are estimates. They may contain subjectivity, prediction error, or bias.

But no disclosure is also costly

Without MA reporting, investors fill the gap with incomplete assumptions or underweight the long-term earnings effect of marketing assets.

Mechanism result

Reported MA values serve as an anchor for expectations, so stock prices better reflect actual future performance.

Core inference

The paper’s logic is not that valuation is perfect. It is that, on average, an informed estimate is more useful than a complete information gap.

This is why the paper finds a positive effect on stock price informativeness despite explicit concerns about valuation reliability.
5 / 7
Contingency mechanism

Why are the effects not strongest for marketing-intensive firms?

Counterintuitive finding

The paper argues that firms with the largest marketing assets do not necessarily gain the most from MA accountability.

Already presentMore internal attention to marketing, stronger measurement systems, and more external scrutiny.
→
Marginal value fallsMA reporting adds less new information when the information environment is already rich.
Additional issueLarger marketing assets also make valuation reliability concerns more consequential.
→
OutcomeThe net benefit of MA accountability is weaker for marketing-intensive firms.
More ≠ More

Having more marketing assets does not automatically mean that accountability has a stronger incremental effect.

6 / 7
Takeaway

The paper’s mechanism is a disclosure → attention → valuation chain

Reporting marketing asset value matters because it changes both how the firm manages marketing and how investors interpret the firm’s future performance.

Managerial mechanism

Disclosure creates measurement discipline and shifts managerial focus toward long-term value creation.

Investor mechanism

Disclosure reduces uncertainty, offers a forward-looking benchmark, and improves pricing quality.

Overall implication

Marketing accountability is not just about proving value after the fact. It actively reshapes decision making and market evaluation.

Closing line: once marketing assets are formally reported, they become more governable inside the firm and more legible outside the firm.
